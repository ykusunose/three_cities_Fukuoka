Three Cities in Fukuoka, Japan

About this area and map:

The Japanese Prefecture of Fukuoka is the northern-most prefecture on the southern-most of the four 'big' islands of Japan. Fukuoka, being one of the geographically closest points to the Korean Peninsula, has played an historically important role in trade with Korea (and therefore China, as well). You can see all this by zooming out. Fukuoka is also my mother's ancestral home. The markers show where some of my family members live.

The photo (right) depicts one of the three Shinto shrines in Munakata that comprises Munakata Taisha, a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
